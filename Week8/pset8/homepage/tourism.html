Tourism

Due to the whirlwind of possibilities that the capital of São Paulo offers to tourists, how to select the best tourist spots in São Paulo? Also known as the stone jungle, because of its tall buildings, it is the largest Brazilian metropolis with more than 12 million inhabitants. Remembered by the restless nights and its cultural diversity. Sampa also surprises its own residents, as there will always be something new to discover. São Paulo offers tourists a multitude of cultural and entertainment programs. There are so many options that this way it seems difficult to put together a São Paulo script, right? Do not worry! The tours are for all tastes, such as museums, theaters, bars, restaurants and shows. Among them, we have separated 15 mandatory stops for you to make in the capital.

Use the arrows to navigate between locations. The titles contain a link to a translated page with more information about each location.

MASP - Museu de Arte de São Paulo Assis Chateaubriand

The Museum of Art of São Paulo or popularly known as MASP is one of the most important cultural institutions in Brazil. Located in the heart of São Paulo, it is a symbol of the city of São Paulo and an architectural reference made by Lina Bo Bardi. Founded in 1947, the museum has a collection of 10,000 works, but not all have already been exhibited at MASP, such as a copper and steel armor from 1480. To get a sense of how important MASP is to world art, the museum is one of four institutions around the world that has a complete collection by Edgar Degas. These are 73 bronze sculptures. MASP is still part of the Clube dos 19. A group of 19 renowned museums around the world.

Catedral da Sé

Catedral da Sé is the best known church in São Paulo. Regardless of religion, the visit is a must. With its imposing construction, it also marks the city center, at Praça da Sé. Its architecture is dazzling! Surrounded by stained glass, mosaics and sacred works of art, it has a capacity for 8,000 people, being considered the largest in South America.

Pinacoteca de São Paulo

The Pinacoteca do Estado de São Paulo is a true work of art. The building built at the end of the 19th century has exposed bricks in its architecture, which gives it more charm. It is the oldest art museum in São Paulo, founded on December 24, 1905, and established as a state museum since 1911. There you will find a collection with more than 9,000 works of art. Among them are collections by Brazilian artists such as Benedito Calixto, Eliseu Visconti, Portinari and Anita Malfatti. After the tour through the history of Brazil, you can visit the Pinacoteca annex building, which has temporary exhibitions.

Mercadão - Mercado Municipal de São Paulo

Visiting the Municipal Market of São Paulo is a true encounter with gastronomy from around the world. If you are the team that likes to experiment before buying the products, you will be in paradise! The “Mercadão” is the perfect place to taste that different fruit and eat that cheese that you can't even pronounce the name properly. Don't leave without trying the traditional bologna sandwich. The delicacy comes to have more than 300 grams of mortadella in a roll. After this stop you will be more than prepared to withstand the rush of São Paulo.

Parque Ibirapuera

Ibirapuera Park is one of the most famous in São Paulo. Considered the backyard of São Paulo, it enchants for its nature and the variety of free attractions. The location is perfect for biking, skating, jogging or picnicking. Visitors also have tour options, such as the Museum of Modern Art, Afro Brasil Museum and the Bienal Foundation.

Avenida Paulista

Paulista Avenue is the place that best defines the cultural diversity of São Paulo. You need to take a walk there! Rent a bike and get lost (in a good way) along the main avenue of one of the biggest cities in the world. If it's your first time in town, don't be in a hurry! Enjoy São Paulo from end to end and get into the atmosphere of the capital. The avenue offers several attractions. There you will find MASP, spaces such as Itaú Cultural, Japan House, Centro Cultural Fiesp and Conjunto Nacional. Take the opportunity and stop at Casa das Rosas for a cup of coffee in the garden.

Museu do Ipiranga

The Museu Paulista of the University of São Paulo (known as Museu do Ipiranga - "Ipiranga Museum") is a Brazilian history museum located near where Emperor Pedro I proclaimed the Brazilian independence on the banks of Ipiranga brook in the Southeast region of the city of São Paulo. It is the oldest public museum in the city of São Paulo, whose headquarters is a monument-building that is part of the architectural complex of Parque da Independência. It is the most important museum at the University of São Paulo and one of the most visited in the city of São Paulo. It contains a huge collection of furniture, documents and historically relevant artwork, especially relating to the Brazilian Empire era.
